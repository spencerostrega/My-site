My Story

The "It Can Always Be Worse" Advantage

Growing up in the foster care system, I learned a fundamental truth very early on: It can always be worse.

To some, that sounds like pessimism. They hear those words and assume it means giving up or settling for less. To me, it’s a superpower. It means that no matter how deep the hole seems, there is a foothold. There is always a positive element to highlight, a lesson to extract, and a foundation to build on. You just have to be willing to strip away the noise and look for it.

From the System to the Stage

I’ve lived this reality. I know what it takes to look at a stacked deck and figure out how to play the hand anyway. Navigating the system requires resilience, but thriving outside of it requires strategy. I took the "survival" skills I learned early on and translated them into professional growth strategies.

It was the highest honor of my life to take the stage as the Honorary Guest Speaker for the Older Youth Services high school congratulations party. Looking out at a graduating class of foster youth, I didn't give them a generic "follow your dreams" speech. I gave them reality. I congratulated them for doing what many assumed they couldn't, and I challenged them to use their unique perspective as an advantage in the real world.

Whether I'm working as a Lead Growth Strategist, recruiting top talent for the skilled trades, or counseling young adults on alternative career paths, my mission remains the same. I want to take that exact mentality—grit, perspective, and strategic action—and teach it to organizations, students, and professionals who are ready to build something real.
